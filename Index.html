Chronic Kidney Diseases (CKD)

What is CKD?

it is a long-term condition in which the kidneys do not function properly, leading to impaired functionality. Various conditions can cause CKD. Most of the time, CKD does not produce symptoms and may remain undetectable until it has reached an advanced stage. Additionally, CKD may not necessarily progress to kidney failure. However, patients at any stage of CKD face an increased risk of developing bloolcardiovascular diseases, such as heart disease or stroke.
